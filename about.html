About Joe Dirt

"Joe Dirt" is a "comedy" "adventure" movie released in 2001, starring David Spade.
It was written by Spade and Fred Wolf and had Adam Sandler's Happy Madison company produce it (an idicator of great quality).

If you haven't seen it yet, the movie revolves around a young white-trash guy called Joe Dirt.
He tells his story on a radio show and becomes a media sensation.
Listeners are rooting for him to fulfil his quest of finding his parents whom he lost at The Grand Canyon.

To me it's the only movie where David Spade doesn't play a little bitch (Tommy Boy, Black Sheep) or some
desperate playboy ladies-man (Grown Ups, Rules of Engagement) but rather a sweet and wholesome character.
You empathize with Joe Dirt and want him to win in the end.

About the project

When I first saw the movie "Joe Dirt" I thought to myself man, those are some really cool clothes.
I wanted to create a lookbook or something similar but never pursued the idea.
About a year later, while slowly regaining interest in the movie and the web dev department I started thinking of this.
The project uses HTML and CSS by me and JavaScript written with the help of ChatGPT(I'm not able to really do it myself yet)
For the artwork I used Procreate on the iPad Pro with the 2nd Gen Apple Pencil.
Also, since I mostly create realistic-looking portraits of people, doing this more cartoony style was a challenge for me and I'm happy with how it looks.
As of now the project took probably more than 20h (16h for the art).
